How the layers work together

Every scorecard starts with L1 (universals — always present). L2 adds what every person on that team needs to succeed at Cartwheel. L3 adds what's specific to this particular req — seniority, scope, or hard technical requirements that differ between roles in the same department.

Why Mission is standalone in every department

Population specificity — Medicaid youth, under-resourced districts — is a discrete screen regardless of function. For SDRs it's a performance differentiator. For engineers it's a retention signal. For Operations, it's the highest-weight criterion.

What Layer 3 tells you

L3 is where two reqs in the same department diverge. A Clinical Manager and a Care Coordination Manager share L2. But licensure is a hard gate for one and irrelevant for the other. L3 captures that — don't skip it.
